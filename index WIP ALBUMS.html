About Scott

Scott pursued his undergraduate education at the esteemed USC School of Cinematic Arts, specializing in Film & Television Production—widely regarded as the world's leading academic program in the film & entertainment field.

Known as USC's "secret weapon," Scott made history scoring over 100 thesis films, including Cannes and Student Academy Award-recognized projects.

In 2020, Scott's work was showcased on a global stage when he scored a “Shot on iPhone” short for Apple Inc. Prior to graduating film school, Scott was invited to join Hans Zimmer’s Bleeding Fingers Music.

Scott's career trajectory has seen him collaborate with industry luminaries such as Roeban Katz and Alexandre Desplat. He has also worked with major networks like ABC, where he contributed to special editions of ABC 20/20’s “Black Panther: In Search of Wakanda” and “Disney 100: A Century of Dreams”.

Beyond film, Scott has sound-designed segments for Coachella and partnered with top-tier digital creators. His research-backed cochlear implant audio simulation for advocate Chrissy Marshall reached 2M+ viewers, and his editing and original scoring for Chelsea Sik helped drive a channel network of 674M+ views, including a viral reel topping 30 million views.
